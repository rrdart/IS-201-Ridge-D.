The Cultural American East

A Brief Understanding of the Region

From Southern New York to Northern Georgia, this is the rocky bed of the cultural East

Where many of our roots all lead back to, Appalachia has been a region teeming with culture dating back to 1528 when the first indigenous peoples in Florida were found to be named "Apalachen". The Spanish expedition "Narvaez" altered the name to Apalache, then later spelled "Appalachian" is the 4th oldest surviving European place-name in the U.S. This name later became associated with the mountain range in 1562 and has been called this way ever since.

The Old and the New, Appalachia has a history has troubled as its terrain

Until the advent of the ARC (Appalachian Regional Commission), the region was defined by its proximity to the mountain range stretching from the north end of West Virginia down to North East of Alabama. In the year 1965 the ARC was established and the regions borders stretched all the way North East to Vermont and all the way South West to Mississippi.

Amidst the many struggles, the region is a massive camping, fishing, skiing, rafting and spelunking tourist bed over the years. Not to mention the fact that it has become a bustling retirment community for those who get tired on their final migration to Florida

Places like Baxter State park the gem of the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, or Clingmans Dome as the highest point along the Appalachian Trail serve as great pulls for toursits and retiries. The many lakes and ponds serve as great places for avid fishing nuts to discover, many abondoned forest fire lookouts serve as great hiking destinations all along the often dry and humid climate of the mountains. Many also choose to vacation here for the calm, and peaceful environment, having some of the lowest populations per capita. This allows for many to enjoy the many Native American cultural sites and monuments as they were intended to.
